## **Why Smart Businesses Are Moving to the Cloud**

Business technology has evolved, and cloud storage is now a must-have for flexibility, security, and remote access. With more teams working remote or from home, sharing files on the go, and storing critical data digitally, cloud services keep your business running smoothly, no matter where you are.

In fact, [94% of businesses that moved to the cloud said their security improved](https://www.forbes.com/councils/forbestechcouncil/2024/02/12/latest-trends-and-predictions-for-the-future-of-cloud-hosting/). That’s a big win for small businesses who need every advantage, as well as a resounding testimonial for those who haven’t yet made the move to cloud storage.

### **Here’s why cloud storage is so popular:**

- **Affordable and flexible**: Pay only for what you need, with no bulky servers required.

- **Secure**: Most services include advanced encryption and access controls.

- **Easy to scale**: Efficiently add space when you grow without expensive upgrades.

- **Great for teamwork**: Securely access and share files from anywhere and across multiple devices.

In short, cloud storage helps you stay productive and protected, even if you’re not a tech expert.

## **How to Choose the Right Cloud Storage for Your Business**

Your cloud solution should fit your business like a glove. Here’s how we suggest proceeding to help find the perfect match.

### **1. Know What You’re Storing (And Why)**

Before jumping in, ask yourself:

- How much data are we storing now?

- Which files are active, and which are just for records or archive?

- What are the compliance requirements of our stored data?

- Are we growing fast? If so, why?

Understanding your data helps you avoid overpaying for space you don’t need and ensures you don’t run out when things get busy.

**Pro tip**: Different industries have different needs. A law office storing PDFs won’t use the same space as a video-heavy marketing team, but they’ll likely have more sensitive data to secure.

### **2. Think Beyond the Price Tag**

Yes, monthly costs matter, but they’re not the whole story. Watch for hidden fees, like:

- Charges for set-up, data transfers or migrations

- Premium prices for faster downloads or advanced security

- Add-on costs for backups, extra users or additional devices

**Fixed vs. Pay-as-You-Go**:
If your data needs change month-to-month, a flexible plan might be smarter. If things are steady, a flat monthly rate will likely make budgeting easier.

### **3. Put Security Front and Center**

Cyberattacks don’t just hit big corporations. [Nearly half of all attacks target small businesses](https://www.zippia.com/advice/cybersecurity-statistics/#:~:text=43%25%20of%20cyberattacks%20target%20small%20businesses.%20While%20just,small%20businesses%20have%20no%20cybersecurity%20protection%20in%20place.), and **only about 5% of company data files and folders are properly secured**! That means your cloud provider must offer:

- End-to-end encryption

- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)

- Regular backups and disaster recovery

- Compliance with standards like HIPAA, GDPR, or ISO 27001

If you’re storing customer data or working in healthcare, finance, or local government, make sure your provider meets your industry’s specific compliance rules and regulations.  If you need help here, Simple IT can assist.

### **4. Don’t Skip Support**

Even the best technology can glitch. When it does, you’ll want support that shows up fast. Look for providers with:

- Readily available customer service by chat, phone, or email

- Clear response time guarantees and Service Level Agreements (SLA’s)

- Disaster recovery plans to confirm your data will never be lost

When something goes wrong, responsive support along with fail-proof data backup and recovery guarantees can mean the difference between a small delay and a crippling disaster.

### **5. Choose a Cloud That Grows With You**

Today, you may have three employees, a handful of clients and a few dozen folders. Tomorrow? You might need room for 30 users, shared projects, and automated tools.

Choose a provider that help you:

- Increase storage anytime while your operation scales

- Add and adjust new team members, or office locations, easily

- Use advanced features like smart search or file tagging to locate data easily

Scalability and future-proofing keeps you ready for what tomorrow may bring without having to start over.

### **6. Make It Easy (And Compatible)**

If your team avoids the cloud because it’s too complicated, it’s not helping anyone. Look for:

- Drag-and-drop file sharing

- Sync across laptops and phones

- A clean and intuitive dashboard that doesn’t need a training manual

And don’t forget integrations. Your storage should play nice with:

- Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, Teams)

- Google Workspace (Docs, Gmail)

- CRM tools like HubSpot or Salesforce

- Project managers like Trello, Asana, or Monday

Test a few platforms before committing, and ask your team what works best for their day-to-day tasks.

## **Comparing the Most Popular Cloud Storage Options**

Here’s a quick breakdown of top-rated business cloud storage solutions for companies in the Cincinnati and Northern Kentucky region:

- **[SharePoint & OneDrive](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/sharepoint/sites/onedrive-for-business-overview)**: The go-to choice for businesses using Microsoft 365. Seamless file sharing inside Word, Excel, and Teams across multiple devices with robust security and access control features.

- **Google Drive**: Affordable, easy to use, and great for teams already using Gmail or Google Docs. Mostly for startups and fully mobile teams.

- **Dropbox**: Helpful for creatives and heavy project users. If you’re handling large videos, images, or design files, Dropbox does well to keep things simple and organized.

- **Box**: Built for security. If you're in legal, healthcare, or government, Box makes compliance easy and secure.

Each has its perks, so choose based on your priorities: teamwork, security, ease of use, or integrations.

## **Common Mistakes Small Businesses Make (And How to Avoid Them)**

Here are five avoidable pitfalls that could cost you time, money, or your data:

- **Ignoring Security Standards**
Not every provider meets compliance requirements. Always ask about certifications and encryption methods.

- **Choosing the Cheapest Plan Without Reading the Fine Print**
Low-cost plans may charge more later for extra storage or tech support.

- **Forgetting About Integrations**
Don’t get stuck with a system that doesn’t work seamlessly with the software your team already uses and relies on for daily operations.

- **Underestimating Future Needs**
Growth is good, but it’s best when your tech can help to support it. Look for platforms that can easily scale and be modified as your business evolves.

- **Skipping Backup Plans**
Cloud storage doesn’t always include data backup. Make sure your provider offers redundancy, or set up your own 3-2-1 backup strategy (3 copies, 2 formats, 1 offsite).
