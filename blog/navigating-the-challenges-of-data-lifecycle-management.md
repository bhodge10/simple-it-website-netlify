## Understanding Data Lifecycle Management

Data lifecycle management (DLM) involves the governance of your business data from its creation and storage to its eventual archiving and deletion. This is an essential part of data security and compliance management, especially for businesses in Northern Kentucky. The lifecycle includes several stages: 

- **Data creation**: The generation of data, whether through customer transactions, website activity, or internal business operations. 

- **Storage**: The safe and efficient storage of data, ensuring it remains accessible and protected. 

- **Data usage**: Utilizing business data for analysis, decision-making, and operational needs. 

- **Data sharing**: Sharing data between teams, departments, or even with third-party vendors while maintaining data security and privacy. 

- **Data archiving**: Storing older data that’s not actively used but still required for compliance or future reference. 

- **Data deletion**: The secure deletion of data once it’s no longer needed to mitigate data breach risks and ensure compliance with data protection regulations. 

Each of these stages presents its own challenges, particularly for small and medium-sized businesses (SMBs) that lack dedicated in-house IT departments. Mismanagement of any of these stages can lead to significant risks, including security breaches, regulatory non-compliance, and escalating operational costs.
