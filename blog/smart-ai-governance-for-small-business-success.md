## **5 Essential Rules for ChatGPT and AI Governance**

To keep AI safe, accurate, and trustworthy, you need clear expectations. These five rules help your organization set smart guidelines that protect security, data, and productivity.

### **Rule 1. Set Clear Boundaries Before You Begin**

Start with a simple question: where should your team use AI and where should it stay out? Clear boundaries prevent accidental misuse and keep confidential information protected. Share these expectations with employees in plain language so everyone knows how to use AI confidently. Since business goals change over time, your boundaries should be reviewed and updated as needed.

### **Rule 2. Always Keep Humans in the Loop**

AI can sound convincing even when it gets things wrong. Human oversight ensures quality, accuracy, and the right tone. No AI-generated material should be published, shared, or used for decision-making until a person reviews it. The [U.S. Copyright Office](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/LSB10922) also notes that fully AI-generated content is not protected by copyright, so human involvement is essential for originality and legal ownership.

### **Rule 3. Ensure Transparency and Keep Logs**

AI activity should be visible, not a mystery. Track prompts, timestamps, user names, and tool versions so you can easily review how AI is used. These logs support better training, stronger security, and smoother compliance checks. They also help you understand which AI tools actually deliver value and which ones need improvement.

### **Rule 4. Protect Data and Intellectual Property**

When you type a prompt into ChatGPT, you risk sharing information with a third party. That means confidential or client-specific details should never be placed into public AI tools. Your AI rules should clearly explain what data is allowed and what should stay inside your secure systems. This protects clients, contracts, and your reputation.

### **Rule 5. Make AI Governance a Continuous Practice**

AI changes quickly. Your policies must evolve along with it. Schedule regular reviews every quarter to confirm that your guidelines still make sense, your team understands them, and no new risks have appeared. Consistent updates keep Smart AI Governance for Small Business Success relevant and reliable.
