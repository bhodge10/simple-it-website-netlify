Artificial intelligence (AI) is rapidly transforming industries. AI offers businesses innovative solutions and automation capabilities which are hugely beneficial to expand production. But with this progress being put to use so quickly comes concern: AI data breaches. As AI has rapidly become more integrated into our systems, and sometimes without proper safeguards in a rush to gain its benefits, the risks can increase. The data AI collects, analyses, and utilizes becomes a target.

A recent study on AI security breaches revealed a sobering truth. In the last year, [77% of businesses have experienced a breach of their AI](https://tech.co/news/study-business-ai-security-breaches).  This poses a significant threat to organizations and their business continuity. A cybersecurity breach can potentially expose sensitive data. As well as compromise intellectual property, disrupt critical operations and present other negative elements to IT Compliance and Security Audits.

But wait before you hit the panic button and shun AI completely. Let's explore why AI data breaches are on the rise. As well as what steps you can take to safeguard your company's valuable information and IT infrastructure.

## Why AI Data Breaches are Growing in Frequency

Several factors contribute to the increasing risk of AI data breaches:

- **The Expanding Attack Surface:** AI adoption is increasing fast because its benefits are real and businesses want to realize them quickly before others. As it increases faster than security and integration best practices and policies are in-place, so do the number of potential entry points and network vulnerabilities for attackers. Hackers can target these vulnerabilities in AI models and data pipelines to access networks, endpoints and personally identifiable information. They can also disrupt the underlying network and IT infrastructure supporting them.

- **Data, the Fuel of AI:**AI thrives on data. The vast amount of data collected for training and operation makes a tempting target. This data could include customer information, business secrets, and financial records. And even personal details of employees. Business compliance and regulatory standards can quickly become vulnerable and at risk.

- **The "Black Box" Problem:** Many AI models are complex and opaque. This makes it difficult to identify vulnerabilities and track data flow. This lack of transparency makes it challenging to detect security breaches, as well establish vulnerability assessments like can be done with a firewall management in standard IT networking.  Short of robust Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) documentation and continuous logging, tracking data flow accurately in real time can be impossible.

- **Evolving Attack Techniques:** Cybercriminals are constantly developing new methods to exploit security gaps. Techniques like adversarial attacks can manipulate AI models. This can produce incorrect outputs, leak sensitive data and hamper identity and access management.

## The Potential Impact of AI Data Breaches

The consequences of an AI data breach can be far-reaching:

- **Financial Losses:** Data breaches can lead to hefty fines, lawsuits, and reputational damage. This can impact your bottom line significantly, unless sufficient cybersecurity insurance and well documented disaster recovery and business continuity plans are in-place.

- **Disrupted Operations:** AI-powered systems are often critical to business functions. A breach can disrupt these functionalities, hindering productivity, financial transactions, customer service and other key areas of business continuity.

- **Intellectual Property Theft:** AI models themselves can be considered intellectual property. A breach could expose your proprietary AI models, giving competitors a significant advantage through virtual assessment of your model.

- **Privacy Concerns:** AI data breaches can compromise sensitive customer and employee information. This can raise privacy concerns and potentially lead to regulatory action. Data encryption, cloud data backups and multifactor authentication can all help to mitigate these risks and maintain IT compliance.

## Protecting Your Company from AI Data Breaches: A Proactive Approach

The good news is that you can take steps to mitigate the risk of AI data breaches. Here are some proactive measures to consider.

### Data Governance

Put in place robust data governance practices. This includes:

- Classifying and labeling data based on sensitivity with regularly updated documentation

- Establishing clear access controls and privileged access management rules

- Regularly monitoring data usage, data encryption practices and data backup or archiving

### Security by Design

Integrate security considerations into AI development or adoption. Standard procedures for AI projects should be:

- Secure coding practices with identity access management, endpoint management and password management standards in-place

- Vulnerability assessments conducted regularly by third-party operations

- Penetration testing, remediation practice, business continuity plans, regularly reviewed cybersecurity insurance and security awareness training for staff

### Model Explainability

Invest in techniques like explainable AI (XAI) that increase transparency in AI models. This allows you to understand how the model arrives at its results. As well as identify potential vulnerabilities or biases.  Also consider a third-party security awareness training and assessment program to educate staff with regular updates and assessments to confirm adoption, participation and successful implementation of best practices.

### Threat Modeling

Conduct regular threat modelling exercises. This identifies potential weaknesses and network vulnerability in your AI systems and data pipelines, as well the steps mitigate and remediate against them. This helps you rank the needs of vulnerability management and allocate resources for threat remediation.

### Employee Training

Educate your employees about AI security threats and best practices for data handling. Empower them to identify and report suspicious activity. Conduct cybersecurity awareness training with assessment testing to confirm staff are retaining what’s taught so it can be put into practice consistently throughout your IT network.

### Security Patch Management

Keep all AI software and hardware components updated with the latest security patches and endpoint antivirus protection. Outdated systems are vulnerable to known exploits, leaving your data and IT support services at risk.

### Security Testing

Regularly conduct security testing of your AI models. data pipelines, data encryption and data backup. This helps identify vulnerabilities through your data pipeline before attackers exploit them.
